 I would describe their audio aura based on the overall averages of energy, valence, and danceability. If those values are high, it suggests the listener prefers upbeat, lively, and generally positive music. High energy usually means intense or powerful tracks, high valence points toward happier or more uplifting songs, and high danceability suggests music with strong rhythm that’s easy to move to. If the averages were lower, the listener might lean more toward calm, emotional, or relaxed music instead.

That said, using simple averages has some weaknesses. An average can hide variety. For example, someone might listen to both very calm songs and very energetic ones, but the average would make their taste look “moderate.” Also, this method treats every song equally. In reality, a listener may have replayed certain songs many more times than others, which would better reflect their true preferences.

A more accurate approach would be to weight songs by how often they were streamed, so frequently played songs influence the result more. It could also help to look at other features like tempo or even genre.
